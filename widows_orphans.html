Using CSS: widows, orphans and &nbsp;

A widow is a fragment of text that is stranded at the top of a column, while an orphan is stranded at the bottom. Another thing that can happen with text formatting is the final word being stranded. The issue becomes extra tricky because of responsive web page design and dealing with so many different screen sizes.

Below are some text examples distributed across three divs of varying widths containing three columns. The first rule was to set widows: 3; which had an effect on the final column in the third example. (This text is at the top of that column.) The next trick was to insert a non-breaking space, or &nbsp; between the final two words, the effect which can be seen in the first and last examples. The second example did not require adjustments.

The final example is interesting because widows: 3; and orphans: 3; which appears to be restricting the first and third columns to a minimum of three lines of text. With not enough text to adequately fill the columns, the second column is shrunk to two lines only.
